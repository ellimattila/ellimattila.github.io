Designing for Services course project

In this seven-week service design project for the Espoo Cultural Centre, our team explored how employees from different organizations experience collaboration, communication, and identity within a shared cultural house. The project resulted in the Kultsa Co-Lab Program, an experimental model for cross-organizational collaboration built around Labs, shared practices, and small-scale experimentation.

My contribution
  • Designed, built, and analysed the entire survey (89 responses), identifying patterns in belonging, communication, and collaboration.
  • Conducted interviews using design probes and took part in analysis and synthesis.
  • Shadowed staff members to understand everyday practices and hidden structures shaping collaboration.
  • Facilitated a co-creation workshop with Espoo Cultural Centre employees.
  • Contributed to concept development and created visual assets and graphics for the final deliverables.
What I learned

This project strengthened my skills in service design research, systems thinking, and facilitation. I learned how to combine qualitative research, survey analysis, and concept development into a coherent service proposal, and gained confidence in designing for organisational and cultural change.

Guild Admin Shirt Design 2024

This is the official 2024 admin shirt for Tietokilta, themed "Kilta menee pakettiin," which means "packing up the guild."
